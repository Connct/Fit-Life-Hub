How Dehydration Causes Headaches

"Your brain is highly sensitive to changes in your body's hydration levels. When you're dehydrated, the brain loses water, which can cause it to shrink. This shrinkage leads to the release of chemicals that trigger pain in the brain, resulting in a headache. Additionally, dehydration can cause your blood vessels to constrict, reducing the amount of oxygen your brain receives, which also contributes to headaches. The good news is that drinking enough water and hydrating properly throughout the day can help prevent this."
